## Items

### Wispr Flow — Android Launch and Flow Pro Team Pricing Overhaul
**Source:** [Wispr Flow Roadmap & Changelog](https://roadmap.wisprflow.ai/changelog)
**Credibility:** High (first-party product changelog)

**What happened:** Wispr Flow shipped three interconnected product changes in late February: (1) Android early access, (2) a restructured Flow Pro team pricing model, and (3) new collaborative features for business users. The Android launch is explicitly not a mobile lite version — it's described as "the same core dictation system that runs on Desktop, now optimized for Android." The pricing overhaul removes the previous four-tier structure and replaces it with a model where every invited teammate gets a 14-day Flow Pro trial with no upfront payment, no seat minimums, and no domain restrictions. The business update adds Team Dictionary (shared jargon/acronym recognition), Team Snippets (voice-triggered shared text templates), and team usage analytics.

**Key product details:**
- Android early access: full dictation capability (not a lite version), free unlimited dictation for early access period
- Flow Pro restructuring: previously four tiers; now invite-driven with 14-day trial per new teammate, no minimums
- Team Dictionary: shared vocabulary layer so custom terms (names, jargon, product names) stay consistent across the team
- Team Snippets: voice-triggered templates shared across a team — e.g., saying "insert sig" inserts a standard signature
- Team analytics: usage visibility across members

**Why it matters for PMs:**
These changes together represent a shift from a "land with individuals, expand to teams" strategy to a "teams as the unit of value" model. The pricing restructuring (no seat minimums, per-invite trials) directly lowers the procurement friction for team leads trying to spread a tool their direct reports may not have asked for. The Team Dictionary and Team Snippets features address the key gap in voice AI tools: shared context. For PMs evaluating voice AI tools for their teams, this is the first signal that the category is building the infrastructure for team-level adoption rather than just individual productivity.

**Critical questions:**
- How does Android performance compare to desktop? Latency and accuracy for mobile dictation are notoriously harder — the claim of "same core system" deserves scrutiny.
- The four-tier-to-one restructuring suggests the previous model wasn't converting well. What does that reveal about who's actually paying for Wispr Flow today?
- Team Dictionary solves transcription accuracy but doesn't solve the bigger problem: people speak differently when they know they're being transcribed. Does Wispr Flow's data show this?
- Removing seat minimums is a classic bottom-up growth tactic. At what team size does this become unprofitable?

**Action you could take today:**
Review how your team currently handles shared vocabulary in async communication (Slack, email, docs) — if inconsistent terminology is a recurring friction point, Wispr Flow's Team Dictionary is worth a pilot, particularly for product teams with dense internal language (feature names, customer segments, competitor shorthand).

## Reflection Prompt
