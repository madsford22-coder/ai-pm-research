## Summary

One PM-relevant signal today: Microsoft's acquisition of Osmos to accelerate autonomous data engineering in Fabric, revealing how platform companies acquire specialized capabilities to accelerate AI-native product development. Shows strategic acquisition pattern for building AI agent capabilities.

## Items

### Microsoft - Acquires Osmos for Autonomous Data Engineering
**Source:** https://blogs.microsoft.com/blog/2026/01/05/microsoft-announces-acquisition-of-osmos-to-accelerate-autonomous-data-engineering-in-fabric/

**tl;dr:** Microsoft acquired Osmos to accelerate autonomous data engineering capabilities in Fabric. Shows platform companies acquiring specialized AI capabilities rather than building them internally.

**What changed:** Microsoft announced acquisition of Osmos, a data engineering platform, to enhance Fabric's autonomous data engineering capabilities.

**PM Takeaway:**
Platform companies acquiring specialized AI capabilities to accelerate product development rather than building from scratch.

**User problem impacted:**
Users need autonomous data engineering capabilities that reduce manual data pipeline work and enable faster data transformation.

**Product surface area:**
Microsoft Fabric - data engineering platform, adding autonomous capabilities through acquisition rather than internal development.

**Decision this informs:**
Whether to acquire specialized AI capabilities versus building them internally, and how to accelerate AI-native product development.

**Pattern to note:**
Major platforms acquiring specialized AI companies to accelerate product capabilities suggests build vs. buy decisions favor acquisition for speed.
