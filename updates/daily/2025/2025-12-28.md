### Lenny Rachitsky - 10 Contrarian Leadership Truths from Rippling CPO
**Source:** https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/10-contrarian-leadership-truths
**What changed:** Published interview with Rippling's CPO Matt MacInnis sharing contrarian leadership frameworks, including why to deliberately understaff projects, how to recognize true product-market fit, and frameworks that built a $16B company.

**PM Takeaway:**
Contrarian leadership frameworks from a successful CPO provide actionable insights for PMs on team management, product-market fit recognition, and building effective product organizations.

**PM problem addressed:**
PMs need frameworks for making better leadership decisions, recognizing product-market fit, and structuring teams effectively. Many conventional leadership practices may not work in high-growth product environments.

**How to apply:**
The interview provides specific frameworks including: deliberately understaffing projects (to force prioritization), recognizing true product-market fit (beyond just metrics), and understanding what VCs actually want from product teams. PMs can apply these frameworks to their own team structures and product decisions.

**Decision this informs:**
How to structure product teams, when to recognize product-market fit, how to prioritize resources, and how to align with investor expectations. These frameworks inform organizational design and product strategy decisions.

**Pattern to note:**
Contrarian leadership approaches that challenge conventional wisdom may be more effective in high-growth product environments. This relates to the open question about "How are PM roles, responsibilities, and team structures changing with AI?" - as product organizations evolve, leadership frameworks may need to adapt.

Organizational design decisions, role definition and hiring, team structure choices, and process improvements. This informs how to structure product work, define PM responsibilities, and create effective product organizations.

### Perplexity - Native iPad App Rewrite
**Source:** https://x.com/AravSrinivas/status/2001339439193428044
**What changed:** Perplexity CEO Aravind Srinivas announced that the Perplexity iPad app has been rewritten to be native, supporting new features and improved performance.

**PM Takeaway:**
The decision to rewrite an iPad app as native (rather than using a web wrapper or cross-platform framework) signals a commitment to platform-specific optimization and user experience, which can inform PM decisions about when to invest in native vs. cross-platform development.

**User problem impacted:**
iPad users need apps that take full advantage of the platform's capabilities (multitasking, Apple Pencil, larger screen real estate). A web-based or poorly optimized app doesn't provide the same experience as a native app built for the platform.

**Product surface area:**
Perplexity's iPad app - the native rewrite affects the entire iPad user experience, enabling platform-specific features and optimizations that weren't possible with a web-based or cross-platform approach.

**Decision this informs:**
Platform strategy decisions - when to invest in native apps vs. web apps vs. cross-platform frameworks. This signals that for certain platforms (especially tablets with unique capabilities), native development may be worth the investment despite higher cost and maintenance overhead.

**Pattern to note:**
This relates to the open question "What's the build vs. buy pattern for AI capabilities in products?" - but in this case, it's about platform strategy. The decision to go native suggests that for certain platforms, the user experience benefits justify the additional development cost. This pattern may apply to other platforms with unique capabilities (desktop apps, smart displays, etc.).
