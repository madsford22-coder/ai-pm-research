# June 2026 Research Summary

The product as you know it is being replaced. Not upgraded — replaced. Perplexity just deprecated its entire Mac app to ship Personal Computer, an OS-level agent that controls your files and apps instead of just answering questions. This week we watched three separate companies (Perplexity, Rippling, Notion) each make the same bet from different angles: the future product isn't a better interface, it's infrastructure that lets agents do the work humans used to do themselves.

The pattern matters because it's not experimental anymore. Rippling shipped agents across six product categories in half a year using LangChain. Notion explicitly called agents first-class citizens of their new developer platform. These aren't feature experiments or beta toys — they're architectural commitments. For any PM right now, the question has shifted from "should we add AI?" to "what happens to our product when the user is an agent, not a human?"

## What Matters

- **The interaction model is flipping from passive to active.** Perplexity's old Mac app was a window — you pulled information from it. Personal Computer is a hand on your OS — it acts on your behalf. This isn't search with better UX. It's the difference between asking a tool a question and handing a tool your keyboard. If Perplexity executes even partially, it redefines what "AI productivity app" means and forces every competitor for desktop mindshare to answer: are you still in the window business while others are building hands?

- **Data unification is the real moat for agent-first products.** Rippling shipped AI agents across HR, IT, finance, payroll, and global operations in six months — an aggressive timeline that would've been impossible without years of prior work unifying data across silos. The agent itself is the feature. The data layer is the competitive advantage. Companies that have already solved cross-domain data access will ship agent experiences 2-3x faster than companies still querying fragmented sources. This is why LangSmith's role in the case study matters — observability and evaluation aren't post-ship concerns, they're part of what makes speed possible at scale.

- **Productivity platforms are becoming agent substrates, not just human tools.** Notion's Developer Platform launch is notable because it explicitly positions agents as first-class consumers alongside human developers. This signals where the adoption growth will come from. The platforms that will win aren't the ones with the best UI for humans — they're the ones that become programmable enough to serve as infrastructure for AI workflows. For PMs building agent products, this is a new integration surface. For PMs at companies using Notion, this is the moment to audit which workflows could vanish if Notion became fully automatable.
