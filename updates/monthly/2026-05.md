# May 2026 Research Summary

May showed a clear inflection point: AI infrastructure is moving from "what can we build?" to "how do we control what we build?" The month's biggest signals weren't about new capabilities — they were about governance, enterprise readiness, and the practical cost of running agents at scale. From Cursor's security agents to Microsoft's Agent 365 to Notion's governance layers, the pattern is unmistakable: the bottleneck for shipping agentic products shifted from capability to trust.

The secondary theme cutting through everything: PMs themselves are becoming builders. Teresa Torres ran an engineering sprint to ship AI-generated Opportunity Solution Trees. A Stripe PM vibe-coded Protodash and changed how design reviews work. The skill differentiation isn't "can you code?" anymore — it's "what judgment do you bring to what you build?" That shift has real implications for how product teams organize and what they hire for.

## What Matters

- **The platform layer is the real moat, not the model.** Ravi Mehta's breakdown of OpenAI vs. Anthropic showed that enterprise buying decisions hinge on harness reliability and trust, not benchmark scores. Anthropic's 5x adoption advantage in India and the broader pattern of teams standardizing on specific providers maps directly to this: when models converge on capability, the platform layer (APIs, security posture, integration depth) becomes sticky. For PMs, the lesson is sharp: if you're choosing a model provider, benchmark scores matter less than asking "can I build reliable products on this for 18 months without rearchitecting?"

- **Agentic infrastructure requires explicit governance to reach production.** Cursor's always-on Security Review agents, Microsoft's Agent 365 identity layer, and Notion's audit transparency all shipped in May — each one answering a question enterprise teams have been asking: "How do we know the agent is safe?" The pattern accelerating: security, compliance, and cost controls aren't bolt-ons anymore. They're table stakes. Teams trying to ship agents without sandboxing, approval workflows, and audit logs will find procurement blocked not by "is it good?" but by "can you prove what it did?"

- **The PM-as-builder pattern is moving from edge case to expected.** Teresa Torres shipping AI-generated OSTs, Owen Williams vibe-coding Protodash at Stripe, and the broader wave of non-engineers building meaningful internal tools with AI signals that PM craft is expanding to include direct execution leverage. Max Schoening's framing of "agency over skills" captures the shift: as AI commoditizes execution, the irreplaceable PM contribution becomes judgment about what to build and the initiative to build it. The practical implication: teams where PMs can't code are losing a compounding advantage to teams where they can.
